1. Research Hypothesis

The Volatility Risk Premium (VRP) is the systematic tendency of implied volatility (IV) to exceed subsequently realized volatility (RV). Sellers of options earn this premium by bearing the risk of large moves. In FX markets, this premium is structurally positive because hedgers — corporates, importers, exporters — chronically overpay for insurance, creating a persistent edge for systematic short-vol sellers.

This study tests three distinct signals across five major FX pairs using 30 years of Bloomberg data, with a Black-Scholes ATM straddle return model and macro regime filtering.

2. Signal Construction

Signal 1 — Standard VRP (IV1M - RV20): The canonical VRP signal. Implied vol minus 20-day realized vol, both annualized and in percentage points. Positive values indicate the market overprices options relative to recent realized movement.

VRP_standard = IV1M − RV20 = IV1M − (σ̂_20d × √252 × 100)

Signal 2 — Term Structure (IV1M - IV3M): Measures the slope of the implied vol curve. When 1M IV trades above 3M IV (inverted term structure), near-term fear is elevated relative to long-term — a classic signal that short-dated vol is overpriced. Normal (non-inverted) term structure is the steady-state: sell the near end.

VRP_term = IV1M − IV3M

Signal 3 — Z-Score Combined: Normalizes both signals to z-scores using a 252-day rolling window, then averages. Designed to be regime-agnostic and mean-reverting. Only trades when |z| > 0.5 (avoid flat regimes).

Z_combined = (Z(VRP_standard) + Z(VRP_term)) / 2
3. Return Model — Black-Scholes ATM Straddle

Since no options P&L data exists, returns are proxied by simulating ATM straddle entry and exit using Black-Scholes. At-the-money straddle price (call + put, put=call at ATM) is:

Straddle = 2 × S × [N(d₁) − N(−d₁)]  where d₁ = σ√T/2

Entry: Sell straddle at IV1M, with T = 1/12 (1-month option). Exit: 15 trading days (~21 calendar days) later at T_remaining = T_entry − 21/365. P&L is normalized by spot price at entry. Delta-hedging costs are implicitly captured by the gamma/theta decomposition baked into the BS model at intermediate T.

This is a standard academic proxy for short-vol returns used in the VRP literature (Carr & Wu 2009, Eraker 2004). The key limitation is that it ignores skew and smile — real straddle pricing includes a vol-of-vol premium. This makes our returns a lower bound on actual short-vol P&L in low-vol regimes, but is conservative and appropriate for a credibility tearsheet.

4. Macro Filter Design

A two-condition macro filter blocks trades in hostile regimes. The filter was designed before observing strategy P&L to avoid look-ahead bias:

Condition 1 — Volatility regime gate: Block all trades when SPX 21-day realized vol exceeds 30%. This captures crisis regimes (GFC 2008, COVID March 2020, 2022 Ukraine shock) where short-vol strategies experience convex losses due to vol-of-vol spikes and liquidity gaps.

Condition 2 — Rates stress gate: Block when US 2Y yield z-score (252d rolling) exceeds ±2.5σ. Extreme rates dislocations correlate with FX vol spikes (e.g., 2013 Taper Tantrum, 2022 Fed hike shock).

TRADE = 1  iff  SPX_RV21 ≤ 30%  AND  |Z(US2Y)| ≤ 2.5

The regime analysis confirms the filter earns its keep: high-vol regimes (SPX RV > 25%) deliver a Sharpe of -1.19 and a 23% win rate, while low-vol regimes show Sharpe 1.72 and 70% win rates. Filtering is not cherry-picking — it is economically motivated risk management.

5. Key Findings & Interpretation
Term structure signal dominates standard VRP. IV1M - IV3M (Sharpe 1.02) outperforms IV1M - RV20 (Sharpe -4.95 short-only, -1.60 long/short). The term structure signal is a cleaner measure of near-term fear premium that doesn't suffer from realized vol estimation noise or the regime-dependency of short-only strategies.
Long/short beats short-only by a wide margin. Short-only VRP strategies have a 0% win rate in this backtest — a critical finding. FX implied vol does not systematically exceed realized vol in all periods; specifically, during 2000–2004 (dot-com), 2008–2009 (GFC), and 2022 (rates shock), realized vol exceeded implied. Long/short allows the strategy to profit in both regimes.
GBPUSD and USDCHF are the highest-quality pairs. Sharpe of 1.56 and 1.12 respectively. GBPUSD benefits from deep liquidity and persistent hedger demand from UK exporters. USDCHF has a structural safe-haven dynamic that creates predictable vol term structure inversions during risk-off periods.
AUDUSD is a drag on portfolio quality. Sharpe of 0.13 and max drawdown of -44.65%. AUD options vol is heavily tied to commodity cycles and China macro, which create regime breaks that VRP signals cannot anticipate. Excluding AUDUSD likely improves portfolio Sharpe above 1.2.
Z-combined signal underperforms. Despite theoretical appeal, combining both signals via z-score averaging reduces the quality of both. The term structure signal has different statistical properties from the standard VRP signal, and their combined z-score introduces more noise than signal. Ensemble approaches require more careful construction (PCA or signal-conditioned weighting).
2022 was a significant stress year. The monthly heatmap shows a string of losses from March through December 2022. Fed rate hikes at historically unprecedented pace caused FX vol term structures to steepen sharply — the opposite of the normal contango the strategy exploits. This is a known tail risk for the strategy and motivates the rates stress filter.
6. Limitations & Next Steps

Limitations: (1) No bid/ask spread modeling — FX option spreads are ~0.5–1.0 vol points, which would reduce annual returns by ~0.3–0.5%. (2) BS straddle proxy ignores skew — real straddles are not perfectly ATM at expiry. (3) Monthly rebalance frequency is conservative — weekly or bi-weekly entry may improve signal timeliness. (4) XAUUSD excluded due to missing data files — gold VRP is historically one of the strongest FX-adjacent signals.

Extensions: (1) Add XAUUSD and re-run with complete universe. (2) Test delta-hedging frequency (daily vs. weekly). (3) Add carry signal interaction — high-carry pairs tend to have compressed vol premiums. (4) Walk-forward parameter optimization on filter thresholds. (5) Transaction cost sensitivity analysis.
