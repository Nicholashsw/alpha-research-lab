1. Research Hypothesis
The Volatility Risk Premium (VRP) in FX markets arises from the systematic tendency of implied vol to exceed subsequently realized vol. Hedgers — corporates managing FX exposure, importers, exporters — chronically overpay for options insurance, transferring a structural premium to systematic short-vol sellers. This study examines three distinct signals across six assets (5 FX pairs + gold) over 30 years to identify which formulation best captures this premium with acceptable risk.
2. Signal Architecture
Signal 1 — Standard VRP (IV1M − RV20): Canonical signal. Implied vol minus 20-day realized vol, both annualized in percentage points. Positive = market overprices near-term options relative to recent historical vol.
VRP_standard = IV1M − (σ̂_20d × √252 × 100)
Signal 2 — Term Structure (IV1M − IV3M): Measures the vol surface slope. When 1M IV exceeds 3M IV (inverted curve), near-term fear is elevated — the strategy shorts near-term straddles and buys the curve. In normal contango, the strategy goes long near-term vol. This is the winning signal: Sharpe 1.077 vs -1.48 for Standard VRP long/short.
VRP_term = IV1M − IV3M
Signal 3 — Combined Z-Score: Normalizes both signals to z-scores on 252-day rolling windows and averages. Applied only to XAUUSD where IV3M quoting convention is incompatible with FX term structure (XAUUSD V3M ≈ 9% regardless of market stress; IV1M varies 8–58%). For gold, the pure VRP z-score is used.
Z_combined = (Z_252(VRP_standard) + Z_252(VRP_term)) / 2
3. Return Model — Black-Scholes ATM Straddle
No options P&L data exists in the dataset, so returns are proxied via simulated ATM straddle entry/exit using Black-Scholes. At-the-money straddle = 2 × ATM call = 2 × ATM put (put-call parity at ATM):
Straddle(S, σ, T) = 2 · S · [N(σ√T/2) − N(−σ√T/2)]
Entry: Sell straddle at IV1M with T = 1/12 year. Exit: 15 trading days (~21 calendar days) later at T_remaining ≈ T_entry − 21/365. P&L normalized to spot at entry. This proxy is conservative — it ignores vol-of-vol and skew premiums that a real straddle seller would capture (or pay). The approach follows standard academic VRP literature (Carr & Wu 2009; Eraker 2004).
4. Macro Filter Design & Rationale
Two conditions gate trade entry. The filter was specified using economic logic before observing strategy P&L to avoid look-ahead bias:
TRADE = 1   iff   VIX ≤ 30   AND   |Z_252(US2Y)| ≤ 2.5
VIX Gate: Blocks trades when realized fear (not just historical vol) is in crisis territory. VIX > 30 corresponds to GFC peak (Oct 2008: VIX 80), COVID (Mar 2020: VIX 82), and the 2022 Ukraine shock. In these environments, realized vol far exceeds implied, and straddle sellers face catastrophic losses. The real VIX (v2 update) shows the filter is slightly more permissive than the SPX RV proxy: high-VIX regimes still earn +0.49% vs -2.0% under the SPX proxy — the filter captures the true crisis months more precisely.
Rates Stress Gate: Blocks when US 2Y yield z-score exceeds ±2.5σ. Extreme rates dislocations correlate with FX vol spikes (2013 Taper Tantrum, 2022 Fed shock). This prevents the strategy from shorting vol into rate-driven FX dislocations.
5. XAUUSD Signal — Data Quality Issue & Resolution
XAUUSD V3M from Bloomberg BGN quotes at approximately 9% regardless of market stress conditions (range: 4.6%–23.5%), while IV1M ranges from 7.8% to 57.6%. The mean term spread (IV1M − IV3M) is +7.5% with 100% positive observations — a degenerate signal that always fires in the same direction. This is a quoting convention mismatch: the V3M field for gold likely represents a different delta-strike or tenor convention than for FX pairs.
Resolution: For XAUUSD, we use the z-score of VRP_standard (IV1M − RV20) with a |z| > 0.4 entry threshold. This yields Sharpe 0.17 with -16% max drawdown — modest but positive alpha. XAUUSD is included in the mixed portfolio with equal weighting but noted as the weakest pair. The Jirong framework's superior XAUUSD result (Sharpe 0.41) likely uses a different options structure (vertical spreads, wider expiry) rather than ATM straddles.
6. Key Findings
Term structure signal is the clear winner. Sharpe 1.077 (filtered) vs -1.48 for Standard VRP long/short. The IV1M − IV3M slope captures structural demand for near-term hedging without the noise of realized vol estimation. It is cleaner, more robust across time, and less sensitive to regime breaks.
Long/short is essential — short-only strategies fail. Short-only VRP has 0% win rate and Sharpe -4.95. FX implied vol does not persistently exceed realized vol in all periods: dot-com (2000–2004), GFC (2008–2009), and 2022 rates shock all saw RV > IV by large margins. The long/short formulation earns alpha in both environments.
GBPUSD (Sharpe 1.60) and USDCHF (Sharpe 1.10) are the portfolio anchors. Both pairs have persistent structural hedger demand from UK exporters and Swiss safe-haven flows respectively. GBPUSD additionally benefits from Brexit vol episodes where the term structure provided clear long-vol signals.
Real VIX filter is more precisely calibrated than SPX RV proxy. High VIX regimes now earn +0.49% (Sharpe 0.25) rather than -2.0% because VIX captures intraday fear premium that daily close-to-close SPX returns miss. The filter correctly identifies crisis months while being less trigger-happy in elevated-but-manageable vol environments.
AUDUSD (-44.7% max DD) should be excluded from the live portfolio. AUD options vol is driven by commodity cycles and China macro risk — two factors that create persistent regime breaks orthogonal to the VRP signal. Including it reduces portfolio Sharpe from approximately 1.2 to 1.08.
2022 remains the primary stress test failure. The Fed's 525bps hiking cycle in 18 months created FX vol term structures that steepened sharply (opposite of the contango the strategy exploits). The rates stress gate partially mitigates this but the strategy still loses 8% peak-to-trough in 2022. A dynamic correlation hedge with US 2Y rates would further protect this tail.
7. Limitations & Extensions
Model limitations: (1) No bid-ask spread — FX option spreads ~0.5–1 vol point reduce annual returns by ~0.3–0.5%. (2) BS ignores skew — real straddles have vol-of-vol exposure not captured. (3) Monthly entry is conservative — weekly entry may improve signal timeliness. (4) Equal weight ignores pair-specific Sharpe quality.
Priority extensions: (1) Drop AUDUSD and recompute — expected Sharpe improvement to ~1.25. (2) Implement signal-strength weighting (weight proportional to |z-score| of term spread). (3) Add dynamic gamma hedge for 2022-type rates shocks. (4) Test on crypto vol (BTC/ETH options) as a high-VRP asset class extension. (5) Walk-forward parameter optimization on VIX threshold (currently fixed at 30).
