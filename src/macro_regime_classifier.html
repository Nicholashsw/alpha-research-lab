1. RESEARCH DESIGN
This classifier uses a rule-based two-axis framework to assign weekly market observations to one of four mutually exclusive macro regimes. The two axes are (1) Risk Environment and (2) Growth/Business Cycle. The intersection of these two dimensions generates four states that map directly to distinct asset allocation playbooks.
A rule-based approach is chosen over machine learning (K-means, HMM) for two reasons: interpretability — each signal has a clear economic rationale; and stability — rule-based classifiers don't refit on new data, so regime labels from 2008 remain comparable to 2026.
2. RISK COMPOSITE
The risk score is a weighted composite of four z-scored signals, each normalized against a 52-week rolling window:
Risk = 0.35 × Z(VIX) + 0.25 × Z(MOVE) + 0.20 × Z(CVIX) + 0.20 × Z(HY–IG relative return)
VIX · 35% weight
CBOE Equity Volatility Index. Primary fear gauge. Directly observed implied vol on SPX options.
35%
MOVE · 25% weight
ICE BofA Rate Volatility Index. Bond market fear — critical for FX vol signals and rate-stress detection.
25%
CVIX · 20% weight
Deutsche Bank FX Volatility Index. Cross-currency vol premium — directly relevant to VRP strategy.
20%
HY-IG Stress · 20% weight
Relative 4-week return of HY vs IG credit total return indices. HY underperformance = credit stress signal.
20%
Threshold: 0.5 (above = Risk-Off). The 0.5σ threshold is roughly the 69th percentile of the z-score distribution, meaning approximately 30% of observations fall in Risk-Off territory — consistent with the empirical 22% crisis + 5% wall-of-worry frequency.
3. GROWTH COMPOSITE
Growth = 0.40 × Z(SPX 13W Mom) + 0.30 × Z(2s10s Curve) + 0.15 × Z(Gold 13W Mom) × (−1) + 0.15 × Z(Oil 13W Mom)
SPX 13-week momentum (40%) is the primary growth signal — market pricing forward earnings growth. The 2s10s yield curve (30%) captures credit cycle phase: steepening = expansionary, inversion = contraction signal. Gold inverse (15%) acts as an inflation-vs-growth discriminator. Oil momentum (15%) reflects real demand conditions.
Threshold: 0.0 (above = Expansion). The median z-score — approximately 50% of observations in expansion — is consistent with the 40% State 1 + 5% State 3 regime distribution and long-run US growth bias.
4. REGIME CLASSIFICATION LOGIC
State 1 (Risk-On/Expansion): Risk ≤ 0.5 AND Growth > 0.0 State 2 (Risk-On/Contraction): Risk ≤ 0.5 AND Growth ≤ 0.0 State 3 (Risk-Off/Expansion): Risk > 0.5 AND Growth > 0.0 State 4 (Risk-Off/Contraction): Risk > 0.5 AND Growth ≤ 0.0
All z-scores are computed on a 52-week (1 year) rolling window, making the regime labels relative to the trailing year rather than the full sample. This ensures the classifier remains sensitive to current conditions rather than anchoring to historical averages from very different interest rate environments.

6. LIMITATIONS
No a licensed macro data vendor macro series: ISM Manufacturing PMI, CPI YoY, unemployment rate, and M2 are not available in the Bloomberg project files. These are Tier 1 economic inputs that would materially improve the growth axis — particularly for distinguishing late-cycle (growth slowing with lagging indicators still positive) from early-cycle regimes.
Extensions: (1) Add a licensed macro data vendor ISM PMI + CPI to growth composite. (2) Test HMM (Hidden Markov Model) as a complementary probabilistic classifier. (3) Backtest all existing strategies (VRP, momentum, pairs) stratified by regime state. (4) Build regime-conditional signal thresholds (e.g., VRP only trades in State 1, higher z-score required in State 3).
